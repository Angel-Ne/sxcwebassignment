Nepal's New Social Media Bill Sparks Controversy

Published on: February 22, 2025

Government Introduces Strict Regulations

The Nepalese government has proposed a new social media regulation bill requiring platforms to register locally, monitor content, and comply with strict guidelines.

Key Highlights of the Bill

  • Mandatory Registration: Social media platforms must register in Nepal to continue operations.
  • Content Monitoring: The government can demand removal of posts deemed harmful or inappropriate.
  • Hefty Penalties: Non-compliance can result in heavy fines and possible imprisonment.
  • Increased Surveillance: Digital activities will be closely monitored by authorities.

Concerns Over Freedom of Expression

Activists and digital rights organizations argue that this bill threatens freedom of speech and online privacy. Many are calling for amendments to protect democratic values.

What’s Next?

The bill is currently under parliamentary review. Citizens, activists, and international organizations are closely watching how this legislation will shape Nepal’s digital landscape.
